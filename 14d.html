Classical Mechanics

Newton's Laws of Motion

The foundation of classical mechanics describing the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it.

First Law: An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Kinematics

The study of motion without considering its causes. Includes concepts like velocity, acceleration, and displacement.

"The most incomprehensible thing about the universe is that it is comprehensible."

- Albert Einstein

Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics

Four fundamental laws governing energy and its transformations in physical systems.

Heat Transfer

The movement of thermal energy from one object to another through conduction, convection, or radiation.

Electromagnetism

Maxwell's Equations

The set of four fundamental equations that describe how electric and magnetic fields interact.

Electromagnetic Waves

Oscillating electric and magnetic fields that propagate through space, including visible light.

Quantum Physics

Wave-Particle Duality

The concept that every particle exhibits both wave and particle properties.

Quantum Entanglement

The phenomenon where particles become interconnected and the state of one instantly influences the other.
